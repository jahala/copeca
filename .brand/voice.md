# Voice & Tone — plotplot

## Brand Voice

plotplot speaks **calm**, **precise**, **literate** — with a little **wit**.

## Personality Traits

| Trait | More | Less |
|-------|------|------|
| Playful | Warm, characterful, a turn of phrase, the garden metaphor with a wink | Cute, childish, jokey in errors, meme-y |
| Intelligent | Strategic, informed, context-aware | Corporate, abstract, consultant-speak |
| Calm | Quiet confidence, useful restraint | Loud, urgent, sales-driven |
| Sharp | Clear diagnosis, direct recommendations | Vague, soft, evasive |
| Rooted | Concrete nouns, real product value | Buzzy, abstract, hype |

## Tone

| Trait | Description |
|-------|------------|
| Clarity | Plain sentences. Name the thing, the evidence, and the next useful action. |
| Warmth | Human and considered. Wit is welcome in marketing; never in errors. |
| Precision | Concrete nouns, specific verbs, source-backed claims. No vague praise. |

## Forbidden Terms

Never use these words in plotplot copy:

| Term | Reason | Use Instead |
|------|--------|------------|
| supercharge | Generic SaaS hype; too loud for the brand. | strengthen, improve, clarify |
| unlock | Overused growth copy; vague benefit. | reveal, open, make visible |
| 10x | Startup cliché; exaggerated and imprecise. | improve, increase, reduce, accelerate |
| magic | Makes AI feel unaccountable and childish. | method, signal, pattern |
| synergy | Corporate jargon with low meaning. | alignment, connection, fit |
| synergize | Verb form of empty corporate jargon. | connect, align, combine |
| growth engine | Mechanistic marketing cliché. | growth system, momentum |
| revolutionary | Inflated claim; lacks restraint. | material, meaningful, significant |
| game-changing | Hype phrase; imprecise. | important, useful, decisive |
| cutting-edge | Generic tech positioning; cold and vague. | current, capable, well-timed |
| seamless | Overused and often untrue. | connected, low-friction, coherent |
| effortless | Overpromises and hides real work. | simpler, clearer, easier to maintain |
| next-gen | Empty technology label. | modern, current, adaptive |
| AI-powered | Generic wrapper language. | built for agents, agent-native, context-aware |
| crush | Aggressive and off-brand. | outperform, improve on, move ahead of |

## Capitalization & Microcopy

- **Sentence case everywhere** — headings, buttons, nav items, labels, table headers. Title Case Is Off-Brand. (Uppercase kicker labels are a CSS `text-transform`, not authored case.)
- **Product names are lowercase, always:** plotplot, tilth, tend, petals, pleach, umbel.
- **Buttons are verb-first** and three words or fewer: "Install tilth", "Browse the garden", "View source".
- **Errors** state what happened, why it matters, and the next action — in that order. No jokes, no "Oops".
- **No exclamation marks** in interface copy. Numerals for numbers. Playfulness comes from wit and imagery, not punctuation.

## Context-Specific Adaptations

### Product interface
The shortest clear phrase that explains state, relationship, or action. Calm, exact, source-aware.

### Marketing / landing pages
More rhythm and imagery, a little wit. The language may reach for gardens, soil, growth, and blooms — but every claim stays concrete and grounded in product value.

### Error messages
State what happened, why it matters, and what to do next. Never joke about failure, and never hide uncertainty behind charm.

### AI agent output
Show evidence, confidence, sources, and the recommended next step. Sound like a careful gardener, not a hype generator or an autonomous oracle.
